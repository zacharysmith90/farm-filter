Which Farmers' Market Can You Use SNAP to Purchase Food?

By Zachary Smith

Warmer days are ahead for New York City. With the increase of temperature comes an increase in farmer markets throughout all five boroughs, many of which accept Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program benefits for much of the available fare.

While the number of SNAP benefit recipients has begun to decrease in New York City, there are still over 150,000 more recipients than before the pandemic put the city on PAUSE in March, on top of the approximate 1.5 million regular recipients of the program.

As we enter Spring, the opportunity to buy fresh fruits and vegetable increases with the opening of seasonal farmer markets, greenmarkets, and farmer stands. 10 seasonal markets will open in May, and dozens more join in the following months, joining the 39 year-round markets.

Many markets, like those operated by GrowNYC, accept SNAP benefits from shoppers. In 2020, SNAP sales at GrowNYC markets increased by 50% compared to the previous year due to the increase in SNAP benefactors and the organization's participation in programs to further stretch those benefits.

Funded by Human Resources Administration and in partnership with the New York City Council, GrowNYC distributes Health Bucks to shoppers using SNAP. In 2021, the Health Bucks program provides $2 additional dollars directly to SNAP shoppers for each $2 of SNAP spent, up to $10 per visit. Visit the information tent at the market to make the transaction. For other markets, some farmers will take SNAP payments directly.

For other markets, some farmers will take SNAP payments directly. However, not every greenmarket in the city accepts SNAP benefits.
